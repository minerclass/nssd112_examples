WIG Strategy

How technology contributes without pretending to own instructional outcomes by itself.
Literacy WIG

Technology as evidence infrastructure

Technology should make intervention evidence faster, cleaner, and easier to act on. The right lane is data flow, dashboard clarity, assessment readiness, and teacher support.

Dual Language WIG

Technology as continuity infrastructure

Innovation with guardrails

AI Policy to Practice

A usable three-track answer for AI leadership.

Review

Build an AI-specific lane in the digital resource review process: data use, training claims, age limits, bias risk, opt-out, and DPA status.

Teach

Use short, repeated professional learning cycles. Tie classroom use to policy, academic integrity, productive struggle, and student agency.

Explain

Create family-facing AI literacy materials in plain language and multiple languages. Make approved tools and limits visible.

Careful sentence

"I am interested in AI leadership that is neither prohibition nor hype. The useful middle is policy-literate experimentation with clear privacy boundaries."

Comparison without overclaiming

Parallel Practice

A shareable comparison between BPD3 experience and NSSD112 priorities.

This section is intentionally framed as professional comparison. It names similar work across districts while keeping the tone respectful, evidence-based, and appropriate for a shareable reference.

Trusted Learning Environment

BPD3 and NSSD112 both connect technology leadership to student data privacy. Your best comparison is not "we are the same," but "I have worked inside the same governance category and understand what it takes to sustain it."

AI Governance

Both districts are navigating classroom AI, staff PD, vendor review, and parent communication. Your comparison can emphasize responsible implementation rather than novelty.

Operational Modernization

BPD3 infrastructure, filtering, MFA, device lifecycle, and E-Rate work can be used as proof that you understand the unglamorous systems work behind visible innovation.

Language to Use

"What stands out to me is that NSSD112's public work is recognizably connected to the privacy, AI, infrastructure, and instructional technology work I have been leading. That gives me a useful starting point, while still requiring humility, listening, and careful attention to the district's specific context."
Public communication signals

LinkedIn Feed Takeaways

Interview-useful patterns from NSSD112's public LinkedIn posts and district-facing communication style.

The public feed reinforces a district identity built around literacy, family connection, multilingual inclusion, staff recognition, student agency, and community partnership. These are not side themes. They are the public language the district uses to explain itself.

Literacy Is Public Identity

Repeated posts use #112reads and describe ELA modules, anchor texts, essential questions, and at-home prompts. Technology can support this by making learning evidence, family communication, and instructional resources easier to access.

Family Partnership Is Routine

The posts often invite parents to ask children specific questions about what they are reading or learning. This points to a useful technology lane: parent portals, translation, communication cadence, and home-school visibility.

Multilingual Learners Are Visible

BPAC and multilingual learner celebrations appear in the district's public story. For the technology role, this connects directly to translation workflows, Dual Language support, family access, and equity in digital systems.

Support Staff Are Valued

The district publicly recognizes associate principals, paraprofessionals, bus drivers, administrative professionals, and other staff groups. Strong technology leadership should improve daily work for every adult who supports students, not only teachers.

AI Is About Student Agency

The Edgewood Presidential AI Challenge recognition frames AI as student creation and problem-solving, not just adult policy compliance. This is a strong bridge to responsible AI, student voice, privacy, and academic integrity.

Community Partnerships Matter

Posts about the 112 Education Foundation, Northwestern Griffin's Tale, Art in Bloom, BPAC, and downtown Highland Park show a district that sees learning as community-embedded.

Interview Language

"Looking at how NSSD112 communicates publicly, I noticed that literacy, multilingual family engagement, student creativity, and staff recognition are not side themes. They are part of the district's public operating culture. For technology leadership, that means the work is not only network reliability or tool selection. It is making learning visible, protecting student data, supporting multilingual access, and helping staff spend more time with students and less time wrestling systems."
